Blackberry Pie

Description

Blackberry pie is a decadent treat that showcases the rich, sweet-tart flavor of blackberries.

A buttery, flaky crust cradles a luscious filling of deep purple berries, thickened to perfection with a touch of cornstarch.

The aroma of baking blackberries fills the kitchen, promising a delightful dessert experience.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Cold & normal butter
  • Ice water
  • Fresh blackberries
  • White sugar
  • Cornstarch
  • Lemon Zest
  • Lemon juice

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.
  2. Using a pastry blender or two knives, cut the cold butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to over-mix.
  4. Divide the dough in half, shape into disks, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. In a large bowl, combine blackberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Toss gently to coat.
  6. Let the mixture stand for about 15 minutes to allow the juices to thicken.
  7. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  8. Roll out one pie crust to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Pour the blackberry filling into the prepared crust.
  9. Cover with the top crust, vent, and crimp the edges.
  10. Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F, then reduce the temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  11. Bake for an additional 30-35 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  12. Let cool completely on a wire rack before serving.
